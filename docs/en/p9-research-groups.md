# Research Groups and Lab Management

Three aspects: Resources / Personnel / Projects


## Resources

- Desks/chairs/cabinets/cables/sockets/equipment/phones/others/offices/funding
- Digitization/maintenance/repairs/hygiene/fire and theft prevention/personal safety

## Personnel Management: 6-Step Approach

**Recruitment / Training / Culture / Evaluation / Incentives / Exit**

### Recruitment (Invitation Letters)
Positions: Undergraduates, Master's students, PhD students, Postdocs, Engineers, Secretaries

**Basic Requirements:**
- Foundational courses and other knowledge
- Listening, speaking, reading, and writing skills

**Capability Requirements:**
- Independence / Collaboration / Critical thinking / Innovation

**Attitude Requirements:**
- Task completion vs. self-motivation
- Procrastination vs. advance planning

**Level-Specific Expectations:**
- **Undergraduates**: Meet basic requirements; can work independently/collaboratively when clearly instructed
- **Master's students**: Meet basic requirements; can work independently/collaboratively with some correction
- **PhD students**: Meet basic requirements; can work independently/collaboratively with some correction; capable of critical thinking and innovation through discussion

**How to Test:**
- **Foundational knowledge**: 1) Theoretical knowledge, 2) Programming languages and tool usage, 3) Problem-solving
- **Communication skills**: Accept tasks / Read papers / Summarize papers / Present / Answer questions
- **Independence/Collaboration/Critical thinking/Innovation**: 1) Projects, 2) Assignments
- **Attitude**: Direct inquiry

**Screening:**
1. Above average candidates
2. Three red flags: Poor attitude, requires hand-holding, poor comprehension

### Training
- Fire safety / Self-rescue, psychological preparation
- Foundational courses, communication skills
- Various overviews, technical courses

### Culture: Promoting Team Success
- **Elevate talent / Establish rules / Seek development**
- **Implicit leadership and expectation models** (Pygmalion effect, academic status and mentoring time)
- **Informal activities**: Build friendships and reduce friction / Communicate and inspire innovation / Share and solve problems promptly / Respect employees and boost morale / Maintain pleasant atmosphere and good condition
  - Activities: Lunch / Afternoon tea / Weekday evening sports / Group meeting dinners / Friday TGIF / Weekend team activities
  - Choose appropriate topics
- **Strengths-based management**: Assign tasks to those who excel at them, including both formal and informal activities

### Evaluation
**Key question**: What should I deliver in 6 years?
- Research and teaching + Projects / Thesis supervision / Reputation / Awards / Grants / Collaborations / Department and university interests

### Incentives (Must be clearly identified as incentives)
- Award selection / Free lunches / Student supervision opportunities

### Exit
- Recommendations / Advice
